Printf does not write a newline by default. By convention, formatting functions whose names end in f, such as log.Printf and fmt.Errorf, use the formatting rules of fmt.Printf, whereas those whose names end in ln follow Println, formatting their arguments as if by %v, followed by a newline. Many programs read either from their standard input, as above, or from a sequence of named files.
